Returns! What You Need to Know...

When a customer's item is returned, it is important to know what steps to take to process the return. Do you know how they paid? Was this a Net 30? If so, has that invoice been paid-in-full or is it still open? All of these questions are important to answer before processing the return.

Shopify: If the order was paid in Shopify with either Shop or PayPal, then the refund must be processed in the same program. Remember that no refund can be given in the Shopify system until the business day after the purchase was made. Refunds are also not allowed for purchased that occurred over 6-months prior. This is due to the Shopify system process payouts at the end of the day. If a customer was charged for an item that did not ship, the shipping department will send a image of the picking sheet that contains the out-of-stock item with the word refund written next to the SKU number. The image must also contain the order number to allow the Operations department to identify the shipment in the system.

Manual or Payable Invoice: If the customer was emailed a payable invoice or the payment was entered manual in the authorization software. The refund must be processed within the same system. The same 6-month limit applies to refunds in the authorization system. For out-of-stock items a refund maybe processed the same day, by making an adjustment to the order total. However, the system updates periodically throughout the day, and the shortage needs to be reported to Operations as soon as it is noticed.

Net 30: If the customer has a Net 30 account, the refund must be processed in the invoicing software. If the invoice is still open, it will be adjusted to remove the item. The Sales Rep and the customer must both be notified of the shortage and the invoice adjustment. If the invoice is paid, then a credit note shall be created and sent to the customer. Credit Notes must not be applied to open invoices, even if an exchange is being processed. This will cause the invoice amount due to be lowered caused shrinkage.

What to Look For

While most orders are treated the same there are somethings that might make the order require a different treatment. This is why we stress entering and reading the order notes. Every employee should check the notes section of the orders they are working on. Notes can provide valuable information about payment types, shipment requirements, or special circumstances surrounding the order.

Below are some examples of special order requirements and where they can be found in the system

In this image, we see the order notes from a Shopify order. The Sales Rep. has entered the Credit Card information under the 'Customer' section (removed for security). The 'Notes' section indicates this order is for NFR and requires Overnight Shipping. The 'Shipping Address' differs from the 'Billing Address' and is confirmed as correct for this NFR order.

In this note section, we can see that this order is a "DO NOT SHIP - Pick up" order type. When we process this order we will have to mark it with a "DO NOT SHIP" Tag in the warehouse software Shipstation. Information like this should always be communicated within the notes, and it is everyone responsibility to read the notes and process the order accordingly.

Welcome to the American Darling Training Center

Welcome to the order processing overview for American Darling, LLC. At American Darling, we strive to provide a seamless experience for our customers, from the moment they place an order to the final delivery of their products. Our efficient systems ensure that every step of the order lifecycle is meticulously managed. We utilize three key platforms—Shopify, Cin7, and Shipstation—to handle order processing, RMA requests, refunds, exchanges, and strap replacements for bags with broken straps.

Our process is built on a one-way flow of information, which is crucial for maintaining accuracy and efficiency. Orders are initiated in Shopify, where they are created and managed. Once an order is confirmed, it seamlessly transitions to Cin7, where invoicing and accounting take place. Following this, the order is sent to Shipstation for warehouse picking and shipping. It's important to note that this flow is one-time only: from Shopify to Cin7, and then from Cin7 to Shipstation. The final step involves updating the order status back to Shopify for fulfillment marking.



Overview of the Order Process

For our sales team, understanding this process is vital. If any changes need to be made to an order, they must be manually updated in both Shopify and Cin7. Once an order has moved from Shopify to Cin7, it will not automatically update, making manual intervention critical for maintaining order accuracy. This system ensures that our operations run smoothly, allowing us to focus on providing exceptional service to our customers. We hope this overview provides clarity on our order processing workflow and equips you with the knowledge needed to navigate our systems effectively.

Summary of the Order Processing Steps

Step Number Step Why
1 Access the order in Shopify This is where the order starts and can tell you a lot about it!
2 Review the order comments/notes The comments section can tell you about the payments made and who entered the order. The order notes can give you information about how to process the order (i.e., payment information, shipping information, backorder requests, and purchase order information).
3 Review the customer notes Sometimes the customer's file has information that we need to use for all their orders. We try to save payment information in the customer notes.
4 Open the order in Cin7 This is where we will process the order for picking and shipment.
5 Review the order's shipping notes, comments We need to verify that special instructions appear in the correct locations, like shipping instructions for example. We also need to make sure that payment information such as credit card numbers are not in the shipping instructions. If a PO number is required for a purchase, it should appear in the shipping notes for the order.
6 Review the shipping address Because the shipping address displayed in the order's drop-down section is the address that will be sent to the shipping software.
7 Verify the stock quantities of items before adding them to invoice/pick For manual orders, we don't want to charge the customer for items we do not have in stock. For website orders, we don't want to pick/pack items we do not have in stock.
8 Verify payment You cannot process the pick, pack, and shipment of an order that is not paid (unless it is Net 30).
9 Remove the default "manual payment line" If the order was marked paid manually in Shopify, a default payment line will appear in Cin7. Payment lines must be accurate!
10 Process payments if needed If the order has not been paid, a payment will need to be added prior to shipment.
11 Pick and AUTHORIZE This allows the system to check the stock availability of the items being ordered.
12 Pack and AUTHORIZE This allows the system to generate the packing list for the order; it must match the pick list.
13 Ship and SAVE SAVE SAVE We need to send the order to Shipstation so it can be processed by the warehouse. You must SAVE the Ship process, NEVER Authorize. This tells the system that the order is finalized and doesn't need to be processed for shipment.
14 Update Shipstation This will load the order into the shipping software and allow it to be processed.
15 Tag the order The tags tell the warehouse how to process the order. All paid orders get tagged "AMBER APPROVED", special instructions get tagged "See Office" and overnight orders are tagged "Overnight $ to office".
16 Check shipping notes The notes in Shipstation communicate to the warehouse and the customer important information. We also have to check that shipping notes in Shipstation don't contain sensitive information as it appears on the packing slips placed in the shipment.
17 Tell somebody! If you have a special instruction on the order, you need to tell somebody in the warehouse both in WhatsApp and in person. You also need to mark the pick slip with special notes so the person picking the order will know how to process it.

By following these steps you will have the bet outcome not only for the company, but also for the customer. Our goal is to insure that our customers expectations are exceeded with every purchase they make. This means we all have to follow the same process to insure that the customer is happy with their purchase. We also must insure the accuracy of all the transactions we process. Failing to follow the steps above will result in unnecessary company expenses. For that reason, the order processes outlined in this site will maximize company profits and insure job security.
